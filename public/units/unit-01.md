## Unidad 1: ¿Esto es chido?

**Setting:** Carolina, 26, from Cádiz (Spain), arrives in Mexico City to start a one-year trip through Latin America. She checks into a hostel, meets people, and attends a Mexican cooking school.

### 3. Introductions & Identity

| Spanish                            | English                             |
| ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Me llamo... / Soy...               | My name is... / I am...             |

---

### 7. Key Dialogue (Hostel Check-in)

Carolina arrives at the hostel and talks to Alejandro (receptionist). Key patterns used:

- Greeting: "Hola"
- Confirming identity: "Sí, soy yo"
- Asking meaning: "¿Qué significa chido?"
- Explaining meaning: "Chido es como 'cool', 'genial'"
- Asking how to say something: "¿Cómo se dice 'una pasada' aquí?"
- Giving email and phone number
- Asking origin: "¿De dónde eres?" → "Soy de España"

---

### 8. Lesson Summary

1. **Global importance of Spanish** — 21 countries, 500M+ speakers
2. **Formal vs informal greetings** — adapt to social context
3. **Basic introductions** — say your name, ask others'
4. **Asking for help** — what to do when you don't understand
5. **Common object vocabulary** — especially in educational settings
6. **Cultural differences** — regional expressions vary by country
